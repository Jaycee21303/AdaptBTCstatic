Common Scams & How to Avoid Them

Identify common Bitcoin scams and learn concrete steps to protect yourself and your team.

← Back to Education Hub

Last updated: January 13, 2026

What you’ll learn

  • The most common scam patterns in Bitcoin and crypto.
  • How phishing and impersonation attacks work.
  • Steps to verify requests and avoid rushed decisions.
  • How to report scams and protect others.

Common scam types

Impersonation and support scams

Scammers pretend to be wallet support, exchanges, or influencers to request seed phrases or payments.

Investment and giveaway scams

Offers promising guaranteed returns or “double your bitcoin” are classic red flags.

Red flag

Any request for your seed phrase or private keys is a scam. Stop immediately.

How to protect yourself

Verify requests through official channels, slow down before sending funds, and use multi-factor authentication on relevant accounts.

Team policies help

For organizations, use two-person approval for large transfers and documented workflows to reduce social engineering risk.

Tip

Bookmark official support sites and avoid clicking links sent through direct messages or comments.

Key takeaways

  • Scammers rely on urgency—slow down and verify.
  • Never share recovery phrases or private keys.
  • Use layered security and approval workflows.
