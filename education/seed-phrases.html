Seed Phrases & Backups

Understand how seed phrases work, why they’re powerful, and how to back them up safely.

← Back to Education Hub

Last updated: January 13, 2026

What you’ll learn

  • What a seed phrase represents and how it derives keys.
  • Why seed phrases are single points of control.
  • How to create and store backups safely.
  • Common mistakes that lead to loss.

Seed phrases are master keys

A seed phrase (often 12 or 24 words) is a human-readable representation of a wallet’s master secret. Anyone with the phrase can recreate the wallet and move funds.

Standards like BIP-39 define how phrases are generated, while BIP-32 explains hierarchical deterministic wallets that derive many keys from a single seed.

Important

Never store seed phrases in screenshots, email drafts, or cloud notes. Those locations are common breach targets.

Backup best practices

Write it down, then secure it

Use durable materials and store backups in secure, separate locations. Consider using a fire-resistant or waterproof storage option.

Test recovery

Practice restoring a wallet with a test phrase (or a test account) to verify your process before storing meaningful funds.

Tip

If you use multisig or passphrases, document the full recovery process—not just the seed words.

Key takeaways

  • Seed phrases control the wallet—protect them like cash or keys.
  • Offline, redundant backups reduce loss risk.
  • Test recovery so you know it works when needed.
