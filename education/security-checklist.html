Self-Custody Security Checklist

A practical checklist for protecting keys, devices, and recovery paths when you hold your own bitcoin.

← Back to Education Hub

Last updated: January 13, 2026

What you’ll learn

  • Core steps to protect private keys and backups.
  • Device and account hardening practices.
  • Operational habits that prevent common losses.
  • How to plan for recovery and access continuity.

Checklist essentials

Protect your keys

Use a trusted wallet, verify software sources, and keep private keys offline whenever possible. Never share seed phrases or private keys.

Backups and redundancy

Create secure, redundant backups stored in separate physical locations. Test recovery procedures before you need them.

Security warning

Scammers often impersonate wallet support or exchanges to request seed phrases. Legitimate services will never ask for them.

Device hygiene

Keep operating systems updated, use dedicated devices for custody when possible, and enable multi-factor authentication on any accounts tied to purchases or transfers.

Operational habits

Double-check addresses, use small test transactions, and avoid public Wi-Fi when moving large amounts.

Tip

Write a simple recovery plan for trusted family or colleagues so assets remain accessible if you’re unavailable.

Key takeaways

  • Self-custody means full control—and full responsibility.
  • Redundant backups and tested recovery are essential.
  • Good hygiene prevents most common losses.
