Cold vs Hot Storage

Compare storage methods, security trade-offs, and when each type of wallet makes sense.

← Back to Education Hub

Last updated: January 13, 2026

What you’ll learn

  • The difference between hot wallets (online) and cold storage (offline).
  • How custody and access requirements shape storage choices.
  • Operational best practices for balances you need to move.
  • What risks to watch for with each option.

Hot wallets: speed and convenience

Hot wallets stay connected to the internet, which makes them easy for daily spending and frequent transactions. The trade-off is higher exposure to malware, phishing, or device compromise.

Cold storage: security and separation

Cold storage keeps keys offline to reduce attack surface. Hardware wallets and air-gapped setups are common options, especially for long-term holdings that don’t require daily access.

Tip

Match storage to purpose: keep spending funds hot and long-term reserves cold.

Operational considerations

Access controls

Teams often pair cold storage with multi-approval workflows to prevent a single point of failure.

Recovery planning

Cold storage requires a clear recovery plan, including backups, documented procedures, and secure storage locations.

Key takeaways

  • Hot wallets are convenient but carry higher online risk.
  • Cold storage reduces exposure but needs careful recovery planning.
  • Segment funds by purpose to balance safety and access.
