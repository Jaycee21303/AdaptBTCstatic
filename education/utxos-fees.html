UTXOs & Transaction Fees

Learn how Bitcoin transactions are structured, what UTXOs are, and why fees fluctuate.

← Back to Education Hub

Last updated: January 13, 2026

What you’ll learn

  • What UTXOs are and how they represent spendable bitcoin.
  • How transaction size affects fees.
  • Why mempool demand influences confirmation time.
  • How wallets estimate fees and consolidate outputs.

UTXOs explained

Bitcoin tracks value using unspent transaction outputs (UTXOs). When you receive bitcoin, you receive one or more outputs. When you spend, your wallet selects outputs as inputs for a new transaction.

Fees depend on data size

Fees are based on the transaction’s size in bytes, not the amount of bitcoin sent. More inputs (UTXOs) and outputs increase the size, which can raise fees.

Mempool dynamics

When many transactions compete for space, fees rise. During quiet periods, fees can be lower even for larger transactions.

Tip

Consolidate small UTXOs when fees are low to keep future transactions cheaper.

Wallet behavior to know

Modern wallets estimate fees based on current network conditions and can use Replace-by-Fee (RBF) or Child-Pays-For-Parent (CPFP) to speed confirmations if needed.

Key takeaways

  • UTXOs are the building blocks of bitcoin balances.
  • Fees scale with transaction size and network demand.
  • Wallet tools can help manage confirmation speed.
