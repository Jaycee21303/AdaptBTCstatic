Bitcoin ETFs

Learn how spot Bitcoin ETFs are structured, how they custody bitcoin, and what risks remain.

What a spot Bitcoin ETF actually holds

Spot Bitcoin ETFs are funds that hold bitcoin on behalf of shareholders. Shares represent a claim on the fund’s bitcoin holdings, minus fees and expenses. Shares trade on traditional exchanges, which can make access simpler for brokerage investors.

Who does what

Custodian

The custodian is responsible for securing the bitcoin held by the fund. This is usually a regulated institutional custody provider.

Authorized participants

Authorized participants create and redeem ETF shares by delivering bitcoin to the fund or receiving bitcoin back, helping keep the share price close to the net asset value (NAV).

Tip

Check each ETF’s prospectus for custody arrangements, fee schedules, and how NAV is calculated.

Trade-offs vs direct ownership

An ETF can be convenient within traditional brokerage accounts, but it doesn’t give you control over private keys. Investors should understand that ETF shares are not the same as holding bitcoin directly.

Key takeaways

  • Spot Bitcoin ETFs hold bitcoin on behalf of shareholders.
  • Custody, fees, and NAV calculations differ by product.
  • ETF ownership is not the same as self-custody.
